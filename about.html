Preamble & Story

Who We Are

We, the students of KCA University, inspired by the national vision of Ajira Kenya to transform Kenya into a global freelancing hub by empowering youth with digital skills and opportunities, hereby established the Ajira Club — KCA University.

This club serves as a vibrant campus embodiment of Ajira Kenya's mission, fostering a community where young minds harness 21st-century digital competencies to thrive in the global digital economy. By nurturing innovation, collaboration, and resilience, we commit to bridging the gap between education and employment.

Our purpose is to cultivate a dynamic ecosystem that empowers students to excel in the digital economy — unlocking opportunities in freelancing, gig work, and digital entrepreneurship, positioning members as key contributors to Kenya's vision of becoming a global hub for online jobs and innovation.

Ajira Kenya's 5 Pillars

What We Do

Our objectives are structured around Ajira Kenya's five foundational pillars — designed to collectively nurture digital skills, create pathways to economic independence, and propel Kenya's transformation.

Pillar 1

Skills Development

Comprehensive training in 21st-century digital skills including web development, graphic design, content creation, data analysis, and AI-driven tools — enabling members to build competitive portfolios and certifications.

Pillar 2

Mentorship

Personalized guidance from industry experts, alumni, and Ajira Kenya mentors — fostering personal and professional growth through one-on-one sessions, peer mentoring circles, and career coaching.

Pillar 3

Access to Jobs & Gigs

Connecting members with real-world opportunities — freelance gigs, internships, attachments, and full-time digital roles via job boards, platforms like Upwork & Fiverr, and exclusive Ajira Kenya-linked programs.

Pillar 4

Infrastructure & Support

Ensuring access to essential resources — digital tools, workspaces, and support services while advocating for university-level enhancements in internet connectivity, software access, and entrepreneurial incubation.

Pillar 5

Linkages & Networking

Building strategic connections with local and global stakeholders — employers, Ajira Kenya affiliates, tech companies, and fellow youth networks through events promoting collaboration and knowledge exchange.

Key Constitutional Sections

1
Preamble & Name

The official name shall be Ajira Club — KCA University. Established to embody Ajira Kenya's mission of fostering a community where young minds harness 21st-century digital competencies to thrive in the global digital economy.

2
Purpose & Objectives

Structured around Ajira Kenya's 5 pillars: Skills Development, Mentorship, Access to Jobs & Gigs, Infrastructure & Support, and Linkages & Networking — to unlock opportunities in freelancing, gig work and digital entrepreneurship.

3
Membership

Open to all registered KCA University students (undergraduate & postgraduate). Includes rights (voting, resources, events), responsibilities (70% attendance, code of conduct), and fees: KES 200 registration + KES 100/semester support fee.

4
Leadership & Governance

Governed by an elected Executive Committee of 6: Chairperson, Vice Chairperson, Coordinator, Secretary, Treasurer and Public Relations Officer. Annual elections via secret ballot with one-year terms, eligible for re-election up to two terms.

5
Meetings

Regular weekly meetings on Fridays or Thursdays, 2:00–4:00 PM. Quorum requires 50% of active members. Emergency meetings with 24-hour notice. Virtual sessions via Zoom/Teams supported. Minutes distributed within 48 hours.

6
Finances

Funded by membership fees (KES 200 registration, KES 100/semester), voluntary contributions, corporate sponsorships, Ajira Kenya/university grants and event proceeds. Treasurer maintains records with monthly statements and annual independent audits.

7
Calendar of Events

Semester-based: 1st Month — orientation & membership drive; Mid-Semester — coding bootcamps, mentorship & job/gig webinars; End of Semester — networking dinners, job fairs & hackathons; Ongoing — bi-weekly guest speakers & gig forums.

8
Amendments

Proposals submitted in writing by any member, reviewed by the Executive. Debated at a general meeting with at least two weeks' notice and adopted by a two-thirds majority vote of quorum members. Changes take effect immediately upon ratification.

9
Dissolution

May be dissolved by a two-thirds vote at a special meeting, or if membership falls below 10 active members for two consecutive semesters. Upon dissolution, assets go to similar university clubs or Ajira Kenya initiatives per KCA University policies.

10
Adoption Clause

Adopted and ratified by the founding members on 21st January 2026. Witnessed by KCA University Student Affairs Office and Ajira Kenya Representative. Supersedes all previous versions and shall be reviewed biennially.

The above is a summary of each section. For the complete and official text, please read the full constitution document.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can join Ajira Club — KCA University?
Membership is open to all registered students of KCA University who demonstrate a commitment to digital innovation and personal growth. This includes both undergraduate and postgraduate students. The club encourages diverse representation to reflect Kenya's youthful, innovative spirit.
What are the membership fees?
As per the constitution: a one-time registration fee of KES 200 upon joining, plus a semester support fee of KES 100 to cover operational costs. There may also be reasonable fees for special events, networking activities or banquets, communicated in advance by the Executive Committee. These affordable fees help sustain our programs and resources.
When and where do meetings happen?
Regular meetings are held weekly on Fridays or Thursdays from 2:00 PM to 4:00 PM at KCA University campus. Virtual sessions via Zoom or Microsoft Teams are also available for accessibility. Emergency meetings can be called with 24-hour notice. All meeting minutes are distributed within 48 hours for transparency.
Do I need prior tech experience?
Not at all! Our programs are designed for all levels — from complete beginners to advanced learners. Our skills development pillar covers web development, graphic design, content creation, data analysis and AI-driven tools, with structured tracks that start from the basics and progressively build your skills.
How do I run for a leadership position?
Elections are held annually at the end of the academic year. To be eligible you must: be an active member, have been in the club for at least one semester, demonstrate leadership skills, and be a student between Year 1.2 to 4.1 (Year 4.2 students are not eligible). Nominations open two weeks prior, followed by a secret ballot vote requiring a simple majority. You can serve up to two terms.
What is expected of members?
Members should attend at least 70% of club meetings and events, adhere to the code of conduct (promoting integrity, respect, inclusivity and ethical digital practices), pay dues on time, and contribute to club initiatives through volunteering or idea-sharing. Membership may be revoked for violations, non-payment or inactivity after fair review.
Where can I read the full constitution?
You can read it online or download the PDF directly. It was ratified on 21st January 2026 and supersedes all previous versions. The constitution is also available at the KCA University Student Affairs Office.
